# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local service-business owners (trades, clinics, salons, restaurants, independent retailers, and similar) who are evaluating Omiko as a web design/development agency. They are browsing primarily on their phone, often with several agency sites open in tabs, comparing portfolios, pricing, and overall polish before reaching out. They are not technical and don't care about jargon — they care about whether this agency will make their business look credible online and whether the price is clear.

## Product Purpose

Omiko is a digital design and development studio. The site exists to win local-business clients by demonstrating craft directly in the browsing experience itself: the site's own quality is the pitch. Success is a visitor on mobile who, within a couple of scrolls, decides "these people are clearly better than the others I'm looking at" and fills out the contact form.

## Brand Personality

Confident and minimal. Quiet confidence over flash — precision, restraint, and a near-monochrome palette (already established: near-black `--color-black`, off-white `--color-white`, soft greys) carry the brand rather than loud color or ornamentation. Copy is short and direct ("Ideas, given real shape.", "Simple packages. No surprises."). The feeling should be "this team has their act together," not "this team is trying hard to impress."

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic template agency site (Squarespace/Wix-shaped layouts, stock photography, interchangeable "we build beautiful websites" copy, identical icon-grid service cards). The bar is: a local business owner who has seen five other agency sites today should immediately register this one as more considered, not as one more of the same template.

## Design Principles

- Craft is the argument. Every interaction (scroll reveals, hover states, motion easing, optical alignment) is itself evidence of quality — there is no separate "trust us" section because the build proves it.
- Mobile is the primary surface, not an adapted afterthought. Design and test the phone experience first; desktop is the secondary check.
- Plain, confident language over agency jargon. Short headlines, no buzzwords, no "we leverage synergies" filler.
- Restraint over decoration. Resist adding color, gradients, or ornament beyond the established monochrome system; differentiation comes from precision (spacing, type, motion timing), not surface decoration.
- Pricing and process should read as transparent and low-friction, since the visitor is actively comparing options and will bounce at the first sign of vagueness or hidden cost.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Because the primary audience is non-technical and on mobile, tap targets, contrast, and legible type size matter more than on a typical product dashboard. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all scroll/reveal animations already present in the codebase.
